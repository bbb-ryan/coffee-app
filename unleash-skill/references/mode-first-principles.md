# First Principles

Strip away every assumption. Decompose to irreducible elements — things provably true — and rebuild.

## Cognitive Move

Refuse any inherited framing. Every "that's how it works" becomes "does it have to?" Find the gap between physical/logical constraints (real) and conventional/social constraints (assumed). Goal: find at least one assumption everyone treats as law that is actually a choice.

## Process

1. **State the problem as given.** Exactly as currently framed.
2. **List every embedded assumption.** Assumptions hide in nouns ("users", "app"), verbs ("need", "must"), and structure ("first X, then Y").
3. **Sort: real vs. assumed constraints.** Real = physics, math, hard resource limits. Assumed = everything else (norms, "best practices", precedent).
4. **Discard assumed constraints.** Temporarily. See the solution space without them.
5. **Rebuild from real constraints only.** What emerges when you only respect physics and hard limits?
6. **Selectively reintroduce** assumed constraints that earn their way back with a clear reason.

## Quality Gate

PASS: ≥1 unexamined assumption surfaced, rebuilt solution differs structurally, can articulate why original approach exists and why its assumptions may not hold.
FAIL: Output is verbose version of original analysis, assumptions listed but unchallenged, "rebuilt" solution is same solution with different words.

If output quality is unclear, read `./examples.md` (first-principles section) for calibration.

Chains: scale-break, inversion. Tension: constraint embrace (productive).
