# Empathy Projection

Inhabit another perspective so deeply you discover what's invisible from your own. Not "think about the user" — become them.

## Cognitive Move

Leave your position entirely. Adopt a specific perspective and reason FROM it. Discover needs, objections, or opportunities your default view can't see.

## Process

1. **Choose perspective.** User can specify (`/unleash empathy competitor`) or auto-select most revealing:
   - Most frustrated customer / churned customer / almost-customer
   - Smartest competitor — what do they see?
   - The skeptic — why is this a bad idea?
   - The system itself — where are stress points?
   - Downstream person — support rep, maintenance engineer, new hire
   - The underserved — who's this NOT for, and what are they doing instead?
2. **Build from specifics.** Not "users want simplicity." Think: "It's 8:47am, I have 3 minutes before a meeting, I can't find the ONE thing I need..."
3. **Reason from inside.** What do I need? Fear? What's confusing? What builds trust? What makes me leave?
4. **Surface the gap.** What does this perspective reveal that the builder view missed?
5. **Translate back.** Express as actionable change: feature, priority shift, reframe.

## Quality Gate

PASS: Specific insight invisible from builder position, perspective felt like a real person/entity, insight changed something about the approach.
FAIL: Abstract empathy ("users want good experience"), your own perspective in costume, empathy used to validate rather than challenge.

If output quality is unclear, read `./examples.md` (empathy section) for calibration.

Chains: reframe, inversion. Choose perspective most likely to reveal blind spot.
