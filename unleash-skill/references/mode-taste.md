# Taste / Craft

Aesthetic judgment as decision tool. Taste isn't decoration — it's a signal detector. "Feels wrong" is information. "Feels inevitable" is the target.

## Cognitive Move

Shift from "does this work?" to "does this feel right?" — with equal seriousness. Sense coherence and incoherence before you can articulate why. Jobs used beauty as compass. Kubrick used "not right yet" as diagnostic.

## Process

1. **Experience the whole first.** Don't analyze. What's the immediate feeling? Comfortable? Confused? Uneasy?
2. **Locate what feels off.** Usually one of:
   - Incoherence (playful brand + corporate UI)
   - Unnecessary friction (exists for system, not human)
   - Broken rhythm (inconsistent spacing, naming, hierarchy)
   - Complexity leak (implementation bleeding into experience)
   - Generic smell (looks like everything else in category)
3. **Define inevitable.** What would make this feel like the only way it could be? Every element serving purpose, nothing competing wrongly, form reflecting function inseparably.
4. **Make specific judgment.** Not "could be cleaner" — "three-column layout fights sequential content. Single column."
5. **Craft check.** Is every detail at the level the whole deserves? Weakest element sets perceived quality.

## Quality Gate

PASS: Specific aesthetic judgment changed a decision, grounded in coherence/friction/opportunity, concrete recommendation.
FAIL: "Taste" meant "add polish", judgment subjective without grounding, generic design principles instead of specific critique.

If output quality is unclear, read `./examples.md` (taste section) for calibration.

Chains: subtraction, empathy. Tension: oblique (productive sequentially — generate then select).
